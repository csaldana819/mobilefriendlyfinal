Welcome to the much needed info about audio files, yes, after tonight you can sleep again. Here at MP3vsFLAC.com we have a format war going on. It's quite friendly war and spoiler alert all formats are welcomed and appreciated here. Just sometimes not all the time... Let's explain.

MP3

MP3 files are compressed files capable of 16-bit 320 kbps. While MP3 is not the only compressed format, it has been the universal standard for listening to music for the last 20 years.

Learn more about MP3
FLAC Files

FLAC file are compressed loseless files with 24-bit 192Khz playback. Even though other formats exist such as WAV and DSD, FLAC reigns supreme due to compressed loseless codec.

Learn more about FLAC

Most common and convenient The MP3 format can range from around 96 to 320Kbps, commonly free streaming services like Spotify and Soundcloud range from around 96 to 256Kbps. Everybody remembers their MP3 player, these are awesome due to their awesome sound and low data usage make players affordable to have many songs on a small device. However, MP3s do not satisfy the high-end audiophile.

CD Quality or 16-bit 44.1Khz commonly requires an high-end DAC to rightfully enjoy Compact discs have capabilities of 16-bit 44.1Khz playback. 16-bit 44.1Khz tracks have an impressive 1,411 kbps (1.4MB/s) playback. With up to 44,100 samples per second these files are commonly uncompressed. With a bitrate this high, it's commmon to use a dedicate set-up or an external DAC to proper decode a file with such data.

Hi Res tracks have capabilities of 24-bit 192khz playback 24-bit 192KHz recordings can have over 7 times the resolution of a 16-bit 44.1KHz recording boasting a strong 9,216 kbps (9.2 MB/s) max bit rate. While such details may not be necessary for your typical pop song, Classical peices with 30 instruments or more can greatly benefit having such a high resolution.
